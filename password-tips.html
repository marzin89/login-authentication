How to create a strong password

Don't know how to come up with a good password? Don't worry,
we don't always know either! Our Change Password form is
designed to help you to avoid common mistakes, but it is not
foolproof! Below are some tips to help you increase the
strength of your password.

Length

A good password is a long one. Generally, the longer the better.
Your password needs to be at least 12 characters long, but
there is no upper limit. You can make it as long as you like!

Randomness

Of course, the length of your password is not the only thing
that matters. For instance, "therevolutionwillnotbetelevised"
is 31 characters long, but it is not very safe. A strong
password uses a seemingly random combination of letters
(uppercase and lowercase), numbers and special characters.

Avoid the following:

  • Dictionary words, names or combinations thereof.
    Anything that is easy to guess will be easy to crack.
  • Obvious substitutions. Subtsituting letters for numbers
    or special characters can be tempting, but it should be
    avoided. "H3ll0W0rld" is not a whole lot safer than
    "HelloWorld". It is too easy to figure out.
  • Not modifying enough. Your new password needs to be
    3 characters different from your current one, but we advise
    you to go even further than that.
  • Using your birth date, family names, your pet's name etc.
    Any information about you that is available online
    (Facebook, Instagram, Twitter ...) will be used by hackers
    to figure out your passwords.
